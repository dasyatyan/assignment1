What is included in hike to Kolsay Lakes?

Hiking to Kolsai Lakes in Kazakhstan promises an exhilarating and unforgettable adventure. The trek usually involves a challenging hike through diverse landscapes, offering mesmerizing glimpses of the majestic Tien Shan Mountains that encircle the area. Hikers can anticipate traversing forested paths, crossing crystal-clear streams, and being captivated by the turquoise waters of the Kolsai Lakes, each nestled at varying altitudes.

How difficult is the path to Kolsay Lakes?

The level of difficulty when hiking to Furmanov Peak in Kazakhstan can fluctuate depending on several factors, including the chosen route and the season. Generally, it is regarded as a demanding trek. Hikers should anticipate encountering steep and rugged terrain, potentially navigating scree slopes and traversing snowfields, particularly as they ascend to higher elevations. The altitude itself, exceeding 4,000 meters (13,000 feet), adds to the physical challenge and the possibility of experiencing altitude-related symptoms.
